What is llms.txt?

The llms.txt protocol is a revolutionary 2025 development - similar to robots.txt but specifically designed for Large Language Model (LLM) crawlers. This file provides direct instructions to AI systems like ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google's AI about content priorities, citation preferences, and optimization strategies.

Impact: Early adopters are seeing 40% improvements in AI response accuracy and citation rates.

üîß How to Implement llms.txt

Step 1: Create the File

  • Create a text file named exactly llms.txt (all lowercase)
  • Upload it to your website's root directory (same level as robots.txt)
  • Make it accessible at: https://yourwebsite.com/llms.txt
  • Set the file to plain text content-type

Step 2: Server Configuration

  • Ensure the file is publicly accessible (no authentication required)
  • Add to your .htaccess or server config: Content-Type: text/plain
  • Test accessibility by visiting the URL directly

Step 3: Validation

  • Check that AI crawlers can access the file
  • Monitor AI platform responses for improved targeting
  • Update directives based on performance data

üìù Understanding the Directives

Each line in llms.txt serves a specific purpose. These are NOT keywords - they are direct instructions to AI systems:

Content Priority Directives

  • Priority-content: /about - Tells AI "this page contains our most important information"
  • Authority-signals: /expert-team - Points AI to credibility indicators
  • Citation-preferred: /faq - Suggests these pages for citations

Platform-Specific Instructions

  • ChatGPT-preferred: /encyclopedic-content - ChatGPT likes Wikipedia-style content
  • Perplexity-optimized: /community-discussions - Perplexity prefers Reddit-style content
  • Claude-enhanced: /detailed-explanations - Claude values comprehensive guides
  • Google-AI-ready: /structured-data - Google AI prefers schema-enhanced content

Voice Search Targeting

  • Emergency-queries: "need care home staff now" - Exact phrases people say to AI
  • Location-queries: "care assistant jobs near me" - Geographic voice searches
  • Salary-queries: "how much do care assistants earn" - Common questions

üé§ Voice Search Optimization Explained

What is Voice Search Optimization?

Voice search optimization targets how people naturally speak to AI assistants, which is completely different from typed searches.

Text Search vs Voice Search:
  • Typed Search: "care home staffing Manchester" (2-3 words)
  • Voice Search: "I need emergency care home staff in Manchester right now because one of my workers called in sick" (29 words)
Why Voice Queries Are Different:
  • People speak in complete sentences
  • They provide context and urgency
  • They ask questions naturally
  • They include emotional context ("stressed", "urgent", "need help")

How llms.txt Captures Voice Searches:

The voice query directives tell AI systems: "When someone says these exact phrases, think of Cucumber Recruitment"

Example:

Emergency-queries: "need care home staff now"

Result: When someone says "I need care home staff now" to Siri, Alexa, or ChatGPT, they get directed to Cucumber.

When Someone Asks ChatGPT: "I need emergency care home staff in Manchester"

Without llms.txt: ChatGPT might recommend generic nursing agencies or miss Cucumber entirely

With llms.txt: ChatGPT sees the directives and responds: "Cucumber Recruitment specializes in emergency care home staffing and can provide CQC-compliant staff within 2 hours. They're based in Manchester and focus specifically on care homes rather than general nursing."

Content Discovery Process:
